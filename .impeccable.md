## Design Context

### Users
Flowmail is for SaaS founders, indie hackers, open-source maintainers, and small product studios who already operate their domain and infrastructure on Cloudflare. They use the product while doing hands-on growth operations: importing a small audience, generating one lifecycle email, checking compliance, approving a rate-limited send, reading replies, and approving AI-drafted follow-ups.

### Brand Personality
Sober, operational, accountable. The product should feel like an audit-ready control room for self-hosted lifecycle email, not a broad marketing suite or a promotional landing page.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light, dense, and utilitarian. The interface uses narrow navigation, compact tables, visible system status, restrained color, small type hierarchy, and right-side risk panels. Accent color is used for primary operational intent only; compliance, suppression, and approval states remain explicit.

### Design Principles
- Lead with current operational state and the next safe action.
- Keep AI subordinate to human approval and compliance checks.
- Prefer compact data surfaces over oversized hero sections.
- Use quiet borders, tight spacing, and tabular numbers for repeat daily use.
- Make risk controls visible without turning every warning into a decorative card.

### Visual DNA: Ledger Console
Flowmail should feel like an email operations ledger: every send has source, status, risk, and a next action. The core visual language is a dark cypress navigation rail, pale ledger-like work surfaces, crisp one-pixel dividers, compact controls, and tabular mono data. Green means operational approval or the primary next action; copper/oxide means caution or risk; neutral graphite carries most text. Panels are shallow and structured, not decorative cards.
